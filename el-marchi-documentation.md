# El-marchi: A Tinder-like Shopping Experience

## 1. Project Vision and Core Concepts

### 1.1. Unique Value Proposition

El-marchi is a mobile-first shopping application that combines the addictive swiping mechanism of Tinder with a curated and personalized shopping experience. Our unique value proposition lies in our AI-powered recommendation engine, which learns from user behavior to provide a continuous stream of relevant products, creating a fun and engaging way to discover new items.

### 1.2. Target Audience

Our primary target audience is millennials and Gen Z, who are tech-savvy and accustomed to mobile-first experiences. They are looking for a more personalized and entertaining alternative to traditional e-commerce platforms.

### 1.3. Business Goals

-   **Short-term:** Launch an MVP to validate the core concept and build an initial user base.
-   **Mid-term:** Secure partnerships with emerging and established brands to expand our product catalog.
-   **Long-term:** Become the leading platform for fashion discovery and social shopping, with a strong focus on personalization and user engagement.

## 2. Feature-set Definition

### 2.1. Core Features (MVP)

-   **User Authentication:** Secure sign-up and login with email, Google, and Facebook.
-   **Swipe Interface:** Swipe right to like, left to pass on products.
-   **Product Profiles:** Detailed product pages with high-quality images, descriptions, and pricing.
-   **Wishlist:** A personal collection of liked items.
-   **Basic Filtering:** Filter products by category, brand, and price.

### 2.2. Advanced Features (Post-MVP)

-   **AI-powered Recommendations:** A sophisticated algorithm that learns user preferences and suggests relevant products.
-   **Social Sharing:** Share liked items and wishlists with friends on social media.
-   **Gamification:** Earn points and badges for engagement, creating a more interactive experience.
-   **Augmented Reality (AR) Try-on:** Virtually try on clothes and accessories.
-   **In-app Chat:** Connect with friends and share product recommendations.

## 3. System Architecture and Technology Stack

### 3.1. High-level Architecture

The system will be built on a microservices architecture to ensure scalability and maintainability. Key services will include:

-   **User Service:** Manages user authentication and profiles.
-   **Product Service:** Manages the product catalog.
-   **Matching Service:** Powers the swipe and recommendation engine.

### 3.2. Technology Stack

-   **Frontend:** React Native with Expo for cross-platform mobile development.
-   **Backend:** Node.js with Express.js for building scalable and efficient APIs.
-   **Database:** MongoDB for flexibility and scalability.
-   **Cloud Provider:** AWS for reliable and scalable cloud infrastructure.

## 4. Implementation Roadmap and Checklist

### 4.1. Phased Implementation

-   **Phase 1: MVP Development (3 months):**
    -   Develop core features, including user authentication, the swipe interface, and wishlists.
    -   Launch a closed beta to gather feedback and iterate.
-   **Phase 2: Feature Expansion (6 months):**
    -   Implement AI-powered recommendations and social sharing.
    -   Secure partnerships with an initial set of brands.
-   **Phase 3: Scaling and Growth (12 months):**
    -   Introduce gamification and AR features.
    -   Expand the user base through targeted marketing campaigns.

### 4.2. Best Practices Checklist

-   **Agile Development:** Follow an agile methodology with two-week sprints to ensure iterative progress and flexibility.
-   **CI/CD:** Implement a continuous integration and continuous deployment pipeline to automate testing and releases.
-   **Code Reviews:** Conduct regular code reviews to maintain high code quality and share knowledge.
-   **User Feedback:** Actively solicit and incorporate user feedback throughout the development process.
-   **Scalability:** Design the system with scalability in mind from day one, using a microservices architecture and cloud-based infrastructure.
